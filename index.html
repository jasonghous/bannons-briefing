Acquisition of Desmond L Bannon & Sons

Liverpool beachhead opportunity within a wider celebration of life, at need, and shared bereavement infrastructure model.

Bannons should be viewed not simply as a local funeral director acquisition, but as a potential anchor operating platform for a broader Liverpool based infrastructure proposition and the first live rollout base for the five standardised at need packages.

Why Bannons

Meaningful Liverpool volume, live at need operating capability, and a local base that fits the city where the wider concept is already being discussed.

Why Liverpool first

A local beachhead can help secure pipeline, create proof of concept, and validate the model before any later move towards larger scale acquisition.

Why this matters

It creates the operating spine needed to support a celebration of life centre, future white labelled infrastructure, and the first live operating base for the five package funeral offer intended for wider national use.

What is actually being bought

Immediate operating capability

  • Existing at need case flow in Liverpool.
  • Operational team, local processes, and delivery capability.
  • Quicker route than building a new funeral operation from scratch.

Strategic platform value

  • Anchor operator for the Liverpool concept.
  • Potential base user of a celebration of life centre.
  • Foundation for a wider infrastructure model serving both owned and third party activity.
  • Live operating base for the first five package product architecture.

Liverpool first, then scale

How Bannons fits the wider proposition

If Liverpool works, the real prize is not one acquisition. It is proof of a replicable city based bereavement infrastructure model and a workable package architecture for wider rollout.

Celebration of life centre

The wider Liverpool concept becomes stronger if it is anchored by a real in city operator from day one, rather than relying solely on future utilisation or external partners.

White label infrastructure

Over time, the Liverpool site could evolve into shared infrastructure used by other funeral directors, creating a platform proposition rather than a single site business.

National package launch platform

Bannons could also serve as the first live operating base for the five core at need packages, allowing pricing, fulfilment, margin and customer response to be tested in practice before wider rollout.

Why Bannons helps

Strategic view on Memoria

Memoria may have longer term relevance as a scale platform, but Bannons offers the more conservative first move, proving the Liverpool model and initial package structure before going to the big boys.

Bottom line

Bannons is worth serious exploration because it may unlock a much larger proposition, a Liverpool centred proof of concept for a new urban bereavement infrastructure model and the first live deployment of the five package funeral offer.

Read across from the filings

  • The business appears to have been strongest on a balance sheet basis in 2021 and 2022, when equity exceeded £1.2m and cash was still meaningful.
  • By 2023 and 2024, liquidity had tightened sharply, with cash falling to £8.3k and then £2.6k.
  • Net assets fell from £1.31m in 2022 to £1.11m in 2023 and then £889k in 2024, a meaningful erosion of equity.
  • The March 2024 balance sheet shows net current liabilities of £627k, suggesting pressure on working capital and funding.
  • The company also has secured borrowing over key assets, including an earlier NatWest property charge and a 2023 MSIF debenture covering fixed and floating assets across the business.

What matters strategically

  • This does not read like a collapsed business, it reads like a previously stronger operator that is becoming more balance sheet stretched.
  • Volumes peaked in 2023 and then dropped materially in 2024 and 2025, which broadly aligns with weakening equity and liquidity.
  • This is now clearly a diligence issue as well as a trading issue: the capital structure includes both historic property security and a later whole business debenture, so deal structure, lender consents, and security releases would need to be mapped carefully.
  • The asset base remains meaningful, but it is at least partly encumbered, so any acquisition case should be built around operational reset, package rollout potential, and platform value, not just historic trading strength or gross asset value.
  • Because full turnover and profit and loss are not publicly filed, this slide should be read as a balance sheet, security and momentum analysis, not a full earnings view.
